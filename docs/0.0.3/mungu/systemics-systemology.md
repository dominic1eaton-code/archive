### **1. Formalization of Mungu Theory**

**Mungu Theory** is a **unified framework** for understanding all systems, abstractions, and interactions across **realms, realities, and domains**. It integrates **mathematical**, **philosophical**, **computational**, and **systemic** theories, forming the foundation for **Systemology** and **Systemics**.

#### **Core Components of Mungu Theory**:

1. **Mungu Primitive (Mungon)**:

   * The fundamental **meta-system** from which all systems are derived.
   * Composed of **Mungonic primitives** such as **Impi**, **Ramani**, **Sibi**, **Mali**, **Tathri**, **Indi**, and others.
   * **Mungoni**: The meta-system that encompasses all systems, including **U-systems**, **S-systems**, **K-systems**, etc.

   [
   \text{Mungoni} = {\text{All systems and meta-systems}}
   ]

2. **System Types**:

   * **U-system**: The universal system, encompassing all subsystems (e.g., **K-systems**, **S-systems**, **P-systems**, etc.).
   * **K-system**: A **dynamic** system (e.g., **evolving**, **interacting** systems).
   * **S-system**: A **static** or **structural** system (e.g., **stabilized** or **maintained** systems).
   * **E-system**: A system that consists of **Mungonic primitives** equipped with other Mungonic primitives, generalizing the **Q-system**.

3. **Abstractions**:

   * **Impi**: A generic abstraction for components within systems.
   * **Ramani**: The transformation or **morphism** that relates one system to another.
   * **Sibi**: The cleaving mechanism or the **state** of a system.
   * **Mali**: The **property** or **attribute** of a system.

4. **Dualons and Trions**:

   * **Dualons**: Pairs of systems that represent **dualistic** or **complementary** relationships (e.g., **Ogún-Oshun Dualon**).
   * **Trions**: Fundamental **trilonic** pairs that provide higher-order relationships (e.g., **Obatala-Model** as a trion).
