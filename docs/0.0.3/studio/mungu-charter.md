# THE MUNGU CHARTER

**A Complete Constitutional Framework for Mungu Inc. and Its Ecosystem**

---

## PREAMBLE

We, the founders and stewards of The Mungu Corporation, establish this Charter as the foundational document governing all Mungu entities, activities, and civilizational infrastructure.

This Charter is not a traditional corporate constitution. It is a **living system architecture** — a minimal, closed, and self-enforcing framework designed to persist across generations, technologies, and scales.

**The Mungu Corporation exists to:**

1. **Operationalize Mungu Theory** — Convert theoretical frameworks into working systems
2. **Build Ω-Native Infrastructure** — Create tools, institutions, and products that preserve survivability
3. **Steward Continuity** — Ensure long-term alignment across commercial, cultural, and civilizational scales

**This Charter governs:**

- Commercial entities (Mungu Inc., Mungu Brands)
- Cultural expressions (Mungu Fashion, Mungu Culture)
- Knowledge systems (Mungu Science, Mungu Foundation)
- Civilizational infrastructure (The Mungu Bridge, Mungu Federation)

## ARTICLE I — FOUNDATIONAL PRINCIPLES

### Section 1.1 — The Ω Axiom

All Mungu entities operate under the **Ω Conservation Law**:

```
Σ Ω_i = Ω_total (constant)
```

**Operational meaning:**

- Ω = survivability / kontinuity
- Survivability is bounded
- Local gains require global redistribution
- No entity may externalize cost indefinitely
- All economic activity must be Ω-positive or Ω-neutral

### Section 1.2 — The Five Kora Laws

All Mungu activities must respect these **immutable principles**:

**1. DISTINCTION** — Maintain clear boundaries and differentiation

**2. CLOSURE** — Complete what is started; avoid open loops

**3. SYSTEM** — Prioritize relations over isolated entities

**4. RELATIVITY** — Acknowledge frame-dependence; no absolute authority

**5. CYCLE** — Build for recurrence, not one-time events

### Section 1.3 — Hard-Bound Alignment (Φ)

The **Meridian** (alignment field Φ) is constitutionally protected and **cannot be**:

- Modified by optimization algorithms
- Redefined by business goals
- Overridden by profit motives
- Captured by external stakeholders

**Φ defines:**

- What counts as success
- What counts as harm
- What counts as valid action

**Φ is set by this Charter and can only be amended through the governance process defined in Article IX.**

### Section 3.3 — Licensing Framework

#### **Jiwe Open License (JOL)**

For foundational theory and core canon:

- ✓ Free for non-commercial use
- ✓ Free for educational use
- ✓ Free for personal use
- ✓ Attribution required

Commercial use requires:
- Φ-alignment certification
- Revenue-based licensing fee (0.5-5% depending on use)
- Contribution back to commons (code, research, or funding)

#### **Mungu Commercial License (MCL)**

For proprietary implementations:
- Standard commercial terms
- Φ-compliance required
- Anti-capture provisions (no lock-in, no monopoly extension)

### Section 3.4 — Patent Policy

Mungu will:

- **Not patent** foundational concepts (Ω, K-continuity, Jiwe, etc.)
- **Defensively patent only** to prevent capture by others
- **Immediately license** defensive patents under JOL terms
- **Donate patents** to public domain after 10 years

## ARTICLE IX — GOVERNANCE

### Section 9.1 — Governance Philosophy

Mungu governance is **polycentric:**

- No single authority
- Multiple overlapping jurisdictions
- Decisions made at appropriate scale
- Transparency and accountability

### Section 9.2 — The Steward Council

**Composition:** 7 members (odd number for tiebreaking)

- 2 elected by Federation members
- 2 appointed by Foundation
- 2 selected by Mungu Inc. shareholders
- 1 rotating seat (selected by lot from applicant pool)

**Term:** 3 years, staggered (max 2 consecutive terms)

**Powers:**
- Interpret Charter
- Resolve disputes
- Authorize amendments (requires 5/7 supermajority)
- Certify Φ-alignment
- Appoint crisis managers

**Limitations:**
- Cannot modify foundational axioms
- Cannot dissolve Foundation or Federation
- Cannot privatize open-source canon
- Cannot sell trademarks

### Section 9.3 — The Sankofa Council

**Composition:** Representatives from all Federation Nyumba (communities)

**Purpose:**
- Cultural preservation
- Conflict resolution
- Resource allocation
- Ritual and ceremony governance

**Structure:** Consensus-based with fallback voting (80% threshold)

### Section 9.4 — Amendment Process

Charter amendments require:

1. **Proposal** by Steward Council or 20% of Federation members
2. **90-day public comment period**
3. **Approval by:**
   - 5/7 Steward Council
   - 2/3 Sankofa Council
   - 60% Mungu Inc. shareholders
4. **Jiwe ledger inscription**

**Immutable provisions (cannot be amended):**
- Ω Conservation Law
- Five Kora Laws
- Φ hard-binding principle
- Non-profit status of Foundation
- Open-access to canon

### Section 9.5 — Dispute Resolution

**Process:**
1. Negotiation (30 days)
2. Mediation (Sankofa Council facilitates)
3. Arbitration (Steward Council decides)
4. Appeal to Federation membership (if constitutional issue)

**No External Litigation:** Parties waive right to sue in external courts except for:
- Criminal matters
- Regulatory compliance
- IP infringement by third parties
