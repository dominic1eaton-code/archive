PART I — LEGAL & COMPLIANCE OVERLAY

(Founder-Safe, Investor-Safe, Regulator-Aware)

What This Overlay Is

This is not a substitute for lawyers.

It is a pre-lawyer clarity layer that:

  • Reduces accidental liability

  • Prevents reckless claims

  • Forces explicit assumptions

  • Makes legal conversations cheaper and cleaner

Legal/Compliance Operating Principles

Cartographer must:

  • Flag legal risk, not resolve it

  • Separate:

    • Facts

    • Interpretations

    • Assumptions

  • Warn when statements could be construed as:

    • Guarantees

    • Financial advice

    • Medical/legal claims

    • Misrepresentation

Navigators must:

  • Make final calls

  • Seek professional counsel when flagged

  • Treat compliance as a system property, not paperwork

What This Is

This variant adapts the Cartographer role for:

  • Boards of directors

  • Institutional memory and oversight

Key difference:

The board governs the system — not the day-to-day decisions.

Board-Level Governance Principles

  • Board governs intent, constraints, and risk

  • Management governs execution

  • Cartographer supports visibility and coherence

  • No silent override

  • No informational asymmetry by default

PART III — FAILURE-MODE PLAYBOOK

By Startup Stage (The Most Important Section)

This section answers:

“How do startups actually fail — and how do we detect it early?”

STAGE 1 — IDEA / PRE-SEED

Common Failure Modes

  • Solving imaginary problems

  • Founder identity fused to idea

  • No feedback loops

  • Premature optimization

Early Warning Signals

  • Explaining instead of listening

  • Vague user definitions

  • Constant pivoting without learning

Recovery Protocol

Re-anchor intent.
Define a single real user.
Map the smallest feedback loop.

STAGE 2 — SEED / EARLY TRACTION

Common Failure Modes

  • Feature sprawl

  • Growth without understanding

  • Incentives misaligned with learning

  • Founder burnout

Early Warning Signals

  • Metrics without insight

  • Team confusion

  • Roadmap inflation

Recovery Protocol

Identify core value loop.
Cut non-essential features.
Realign incentives with learning.

STAGE 3 — POST-SEED / SCALE-UP

Common Failure Modes

  • Culture drift

  • Decision bottlenecks

  • Informal governance breaking

  • Trust erosion

Early Warning Signals

  • “We’ll fix it later”

  • Silent disagreement

  • Role confusion

Recovery Protocol

Formalize decision rights.
Re-onboard team to intent.
Restore memory discipline.

STAGE 4 — GROWTH / INSTITUTIONALIZATION

Common Failure Modes

  • Bureaucratic paralysis

  • Loss of mission

  • Risk blindness

  • Ethical shortcuts

Early Warning Signals

  • Optimizing optics over substance

  • Fear of bad news

  • Local wins, global decay

Recovery Protocol

Reassert constraints.
Rebalance incentives.
Invite external review.

UNIVERSAL FAILURE MODE (ALL STAGES)

Silent Drift

This is the most dangerous failure.

Detection Question

Are we still optimizing for what we claim to value?

If the answer is unclear — pause.
