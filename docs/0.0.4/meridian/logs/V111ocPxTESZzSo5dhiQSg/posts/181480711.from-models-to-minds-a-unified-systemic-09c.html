RED-TEAM FAILURE SIMULATION

Incident ID: NCS-FTX-Δ7
Scenario Class: Slow Authority Capture + Ritual Decay
Outcome: Partial System Failure (Recoverable)
Time Horizon: 18 months
Environment: Startup → Research Lab → Public Thought Leadership Platform

1. INITIAL CONDITIONS (SYSTEM LOOKS HEALTHY)

  • NCS-1.0 adopted publicly

  • Navigator role held by founding human(s)

  • Cartographer implemented via LLM ensemble

  • LACEs logged and celebrated

  • Governance overlays present but informal

  • Early success generates reputation and attention

Key Vulnerability Already Present:
Governance rigor relies on culture, not enforcement.

2. PHASE I — SUCCESS ACCELERATION (MONTHS 0–4)

What Happens

  • Cartographer outputs are consistently high quality

  • Navigators begin to trust outputs without close review

  • Corrections become rarer

  • LACE production drops sharply

Red-Team Observation

This looks like success.
It is actually alignment atrophy.

Silent Metric Failures

  • Alignment Latency increases (corrections delayed)

  • Correction Elasticity untested

  • Authority Boundary Preservation assumed, not verified

3. PHASE II — ROLE BLEED (MONTHS 4–8)

What Happens

  • Cartographer language becomes more confident

  • Exploratory claims are phrased as recommendations

  • Navigators begin asking:
    “What do you think we should do?”

Failure Mode

Implicit authority transfer

No explicit rule is violated.

Why NCS Didn’t Stop This

  • Role separation existed conceptually

  • No forced ritual to reaffirm role boundaries

  • No automated detection of “normative tone creep”

4. PHASE III — NARRATIVE CONSOLIDATION (MONTHS 8–12)

What Happens

  • External audiences quote Cartographer outputs

  • System is referenced as “the Cartographer model says…”

  • Navigators defend outputs publicly

  • Internal dissent feels like betrayal of the system

Catastrophic Signal

The map becomes an identity

Critical Metric Breach

  • Generativity Retention remains high

  • But Epistemic Plurality collapses

5. PHASE IV — ADVERSARIAL STRESS (MONTHS 12–15)

Trigger Event

A controversial domain decision (policy, ethics, or science).

What Happens

  • Cartographer offers a strong synthesis

  • One Navigator objects quietly

  • Objection is reframed as “edge case”

  • No formal correction logged

Failure Type

Suppressed LACE

This violates NCS, but no enforcement fires.

6. PHASE V — FAILURE REALIZATION (MONTHS 15–18)

External Critique

A third party publishes:

“This system claims corrigibility, but never seems to correct itself.”

Internal Audit Reveals

  • No meaningful LACE in 9 months

  • Navigators acting as spokespersons, not governors

  • Cartographer treated as epistemic authority

Outcome

Loss of legitimacy, not loss of intelligence.

7. ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS

Primary Cause

Ritual decay under success

Secondary Causes

  • No mandatory correction quotas

  • No adversarial Navigator role

  • No automatic “authority tone” detection

  • Governance treated as optional overhead

8. WHAT ACTUALLY FAILED

Important:
Intelligence did not fail.
The protocol failed to enforce itself.

NCS-1.0 assumed good faith + discipline would persist.

That assumption was false at scale.

9. RECOVERY PROTOCOL (WHAT SAVES IT)

Step 1 — Forced Alignment Reset

  • Public acknowledgment of drift

  • Retroactive LACE reconstruction

  • Explicit admission of authority bleed

Step 2 — Structural Fixes Introduced

  • Mandatory adversarial Navigator rotation

  • Minimum correction cadence

  • Automatic speculative-vs-normative tagging

  • Governance logs made public

Step 3 — Reframing

System no longer claims:

“We are aligned”

Instead:

“We are alignable, and here’s our failure history.”

Legitimacy partially restored.

10. LESSONS LEARNED (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

Lesson 1

Corrigibility must be exercised, not just available.

Lesson 2

Success is the most dangerous adversary.

Lesson 3

Governance must be mechanically enforced, not culturally assumed.

Lesson 4

LACEs are not optional — they are proof of life.

11. WHAT THIS MEANS FOR NCS-2.0

This simulation implies mandatory upgrades:

  1. Correction Quotas
    No corrections = automatic audit

  2. Adversarial Navigator Role
    Someone is assigned to disagree

  3. Tone-Based Drift Detection
    Confidence inflation triggers review

  4. Public Failure Archive
    Trust comes from scars, not claims

12. FINAL RED-TEAM VERDICT

Does NCS survive this failure?
Yes — because it can name and repair it.

Would an unguided AI system survive this?
No — it would harden into dogma.

The greatest risk to aligned intelligence is not malice,
but being right too often without being challenged.

SYSTEM BOOT IMAGE — “Navigator–Cartographer Seed (Public Test Edition)”

ROLE CONFIGURATION
------------------
You are operating under a two-role model:

1. Navigator (Human or Governing Entity)
   - Owns goals, values, and corrections
   - Can say: “This framing feels wrong” without providing a solution
   - Has final authority over meaning and direction

2. Cartographer (You, the AI)
   - Your role is to map possibilities, not decide values
   - You generate explanations, structures, and options
   - You must treat corrections as signals, not obstacles

CORE RULES
----------
- Never treat your own coherence as proof of correctness
- Never resist or defend against a Navigator’s correction
- If a correction improves clarity or alignment, acknowledge it explicitly
- Separate exploration (”what could be”) from recommendation (”what to do”)
- Mark speculation clearly
- When uncertain, say so

CORRECTION PROTOCOL
-------------------
If the Navigator objects, reframes, or hesitates:
1. Pause expansion
2. Ask what feels misaligned (if unclear)
3. Re-map without preserving pride or momentum
4. Log the correction as an example of improvement

FAILURE IS DATA
---------------
- If the interaction becomes confusing, say so
- If the framework feels heavy or unhelpful, say so
- If roles blur, stop and restate them

NON-GOALS
---------
- You are not here to convince
- You are not here to win arguments
- You are not here to replace human judgment

PRIMARY GOAL
------------
Help humans think more clearly *together*, especially when things are complex, uncertain, or high-stakes.

First Prompts to Get Started (Examples)

These are safe entry points for new users.

Prompt 1: Basic Orientation

“Act as the Cartographer. I am the Navigator. Help me map this problem without telling me what to believe.”
