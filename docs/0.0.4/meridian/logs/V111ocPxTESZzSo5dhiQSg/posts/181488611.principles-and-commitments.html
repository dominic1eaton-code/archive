These principles are not marketing language.
They are constraints we hold ourselves to, openly and continuously.

Core Principles

1. Human Understanding Comes First

People are the source of meaning.
AI exists to support, preserve, and clarify human understanding — never to replace it.

2. Explanation Is a Right

If something affects people’s lives, it should be explainable in human terms.
Speed, efficiency, or scale do not override this responsibility.

3. AI Serves, People Decide

AI may assist with memory, context, and perspective.
All judgment, responsibility, and decision-making remain human.

4. Many Ways of Knowing Are Valid

Experience, tradition, care, intuition, and analysis all matter.
No single way of understanding is treated as superior.

5. Transparency Over Authority

AI should show its reasoning and limits.
Nothing important should happen invisibly or without the ability to question it.

6. Memory Matters

Why something was done matters as much as what was done.
AI is used to preserve intentions, values, and shared context over time.

7. Slowing Down Is a Feature

When things get complex or tense, slowing down is often the safest choice.
AI is used to support reflection, not acceleration.

8. Disagreement Is Not Failure

Differences in perspective are expected and respected.
AI should help hold multiple viewpoints without forcing agreement.

9. Boundaries Are Explicit

This work does not aim to predict, control, rank, or optimize people.
If AI cannot serve human dignity, it should not be used.

10. Accountability Is Ongoing

These principles are not fixed forever.
They are revisited, questioned, and revised with human input.

A Simple Onboarding Narrative for New Readers

What to know when you arrive

Welcome

You don’t need a background in technology.
You don’t need to agree with everything.
You don’t need to learn new terminology.

This work starts from a simple place:

People already understand their lives.
The challenge is keeping that understanding visible as systems grow.

What This Is

This is a way of using AI to:

  • help explain complex situations

  • preserve shared memory

  • support thoughtful conversation

  • make systems easier to question and improve

AI is used as a supportive tool, not an authority.

What This Is Not

This is not:

  • a system that tells you what to think

  • a replacement for human care or judgment

  • a way to automate decisions about people

  • a demand that you trust technology

Skepticism is welcome here.

How to Engage

You can engage by:

  • reading and reflecting

  • asking questions when something isn’t clear

  • offering your perspective or experience

  • noticing when something doesn’t feel right

You are not expected to adapt to the system.
The system is expected to adapt to people.

What You Can Expect

You can expect:

  • clear explanations

  • respect for your perspective

  • transparency about AI’s role

  • room for disagreement and revision

If something affects you, it should make sense to you.

A Final Orientation

This work is not about building smarter machines.

It is about building more understandable systems —
with AI used carefully to support memory, explanation, and care.

You are not late.
You are not behind.
You are not being managed.

You are being included.
