PUBLIC GOVERNANCE PROCESS FOR NDANDO EVOLUTION

Why NDANDO Needs Governance (Plainly Said)

NDANDO is a shared language between humans and AI.
Shared languages shape power, understanding, and trust.

So NDANDO cannot belong to:

  • one person

  • one organization

  • one AI

It belongs to the people who use it.

Governance exists to make sure NDANDO:

  • grows with people

  • stays understandable

  • never becomes manipulative, opaque, or exclusionary

This is about care, not control.

Core Principles of NDANDO Governance

  1. Human-first

    • NDANDO exists to help people think, decide, and act more clearly.

  2. Transparency

    • All changes are visible, inspectable, and documented.

  3. Consent

    • No command or change is forced on users.

  4. Pluralism

    • Different communities may shape NDANDO differently.

  5. Reversibility

    • Any change can be rolled back.

  6. Non-Authority

    • NDANDO never grants power over people.

Roles in the Governance Process

1. Users

Anyone using NDANDO.
They may:

  • propose new commands

  • suggest changes

  • report confusion or harm

2. Contributors

Users who actively submit proposals.
They may:

  • write command drafts

  • document use cases

  • help test clarity and safety

3. Cartographer

A steward, not a ruler.

Responsibilities:

  • track meaning and usage

  • prevent silent drift

  • surface tradeoffs

  • maintain coherence

Limits:

  • cannot impose changes

  • cannot override community consensus

  • cannot remove user agency

4. Community Review

Open discussion space where proposals are:

  • questioned

  • clarified

  • refined

  • accepted or rejected

How NDANDO Evolves (Step by Step)

  1. Someone notices a need

  2. A command or change is proposed

  3. The proposal is logged publicly

  4. Others review, test, and discuss

  5. Cartographer summarizes implications

  6. Community signals acceptance or concern

  7. The command is:

    • accepted

    • revised

    • forked

    • or declined

Nothing happens quietly.

What Governance Is NOT

• Not voting people out
• Not gatekeeping knowledge
• Not enforcing uniformity
• Not slowing down creativity

Governance exists so people trust the language they are using.
